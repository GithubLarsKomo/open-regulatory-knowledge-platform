# GLOSSARY.md

## Regulatory Object
A structured, versioned data object representing a regulatory concept such as a claim, risk, requirement, study, evidence item or report section.

## Baseline
A frozen set of object versions used to generate a reproducible report or submission.

## Generated Artifact
A document or output created from structured data, for example DOCX, PDF, XML or eSTAR.

## Traceability
The ability to navigate and verify relationships between regulatory objects.

## Event Store
An append-only record of object changes.

## Knowledge Graph
A graph representation of regulatory entities and relationships used for traceability, impact analysis and semantic navigation.

## AI Draft
A non-approved text proposal generated by an AI system and requiring human review.

## Product
A commercially distinct device family or IVD with associated regulatory identifiers, intended purpose and lifecycle.

## Device
A specific variant or configuration of a product, identified by a UDI-DI.

## Claim
A structured regulatory statement about a product's intended purpose, performance, safety or clinical benefit.

## Risk
A combination of the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm, managed per ISO 14971.

## Hazard
A potential source of harm, as defined in ISO 14971.

## Hazardous Situation
A circumstance in which a person is exposed to a hazard.

## Harm
Physical injury or damage to the health of a person, or damage to property or the environment.

## Risk Control
A measure taken to reduce a risk to an acceptable level.

## Verification
Confirmation that specified requirements have been fulfilled.

## Validation
Confirmation that the needs of the intended user are met.

## Performance Study
A structured investigation to establish or confirm analytical or clinical performance of a device.

## Evidence Item
A structured reference to a source of information supporting or contradicting a regulatory statement.

## Lifecycle State
The current stage of a regulatory object in its approval workflow: draft, in_review, approved, effective or obsolete.

## Object Version
An immutable snapshot of a regulatory object at a specific point in its history.
